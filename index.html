Associate Professor · George Mason University

How does kinship structure shape psychology, institutions, and economic development — and what can we do about it?

I study cultural evolution: the co-evolution of human psychology with formal and informal institutions, and how deep social structures — particularly kinship networks — shape the contemporary world. My research combines historical data, natural experiments, and cross-cultural methods to answer questions that sit at the intersection of economics, psychology, and political science.

I am Co-PI of the Historical Psychology Project at Harvard, a Research Fellow at the Mercatus Center, and serve on the board of ASREC. In 2023 I received the Royal Economic Society Prize.
